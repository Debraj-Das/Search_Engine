Iahub is a big fan of tourists He wants to become a tourist himself so he planned a trip There are n destinations on a straight road that Iahub wants to visit Iahub starts the excursion from kilometer 0 The n destinations are described by a nonnegative integers sequence a1 a2  an The number ak represents that the kth destination is at distance ak kilometers from the starting point No two destinations are located in the same place Iahub wants to visit each destination only once Note that crossing through a destination is not considered visiting unless Iahub explicitly wants to visit it at that point Also after Iahub visits his last destination he doesnt come back to kilometer 0 as he stops his trip at the last destination The distance between destination located at kilometer x and next destination located at kilometer y is xy kilometers We call a route an order of visiting the destinations Iahub can visit destinations in any order he wants as long as he visits all n destinations and he doesnt visit a destination more than once Iahub starts writing out on a paper all possible routes and for each of them he notes the total distance he would walk Hes interested in the average number of kilometers he would walk by choosing a route As he got bored of writing out all the routes he asks you to help him
